Meisner Bots: Behaviour Aware Text to Speech Systems

Abstract:

Humans have a sophisticated ability to interact with each other through dialogues. Machine learning techniques have found reasonable success in generating human-like text for dialogues and rendering speech in natural voices. But the inability of a Text-To-Speech(TTS) systems to emulate emotion in speech creates an uncanny-valley as dialogues require rendering in varied emotions. Also, considerable effort has been made towards modelling the emotional nuances in human dialogues. But the inability of current Text-To-Speech(TTS) systems to generate prosody and intonation for any given emotion lay waste these efforts towards enhancing human computer interaction. We propose a method to compute phoneme level variances given a text and emotion. We show that this method can be scaled across TTS frameworks. We also show the improvement in naturalness of generated conversation when modelled with prosody control. The proposed method improves the naturalness of not only day-to-day dialogues, but also high emotive dialogues like excerpts from plays.
